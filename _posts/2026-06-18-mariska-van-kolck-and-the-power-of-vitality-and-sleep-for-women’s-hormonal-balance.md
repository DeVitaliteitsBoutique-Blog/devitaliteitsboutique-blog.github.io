For many women, including inspiring figures like Mariska van Kolck, sleep and vitality are not just nice-to-haves. They’re essential foundations for hormonal balance and overall well-being.

## Why Vitality and Sleep Matter for Your Hormones

Hormonal imbalances can show up in so many ways: fatigue, mood swings, irregular cycles, insomnia, or difficult menopause and PMS symptoms. These issues can seem overwhelming, but the good news is that small, practical changes—especially in sleep and lifestyle—create real, lasting improvements.

**As a sleepcoach, sleeptherapist, hormone therapist, and stress therapist, we’ve seen how lack of sleep disrupts hormone production.** Sleep and hormones are deeply connected. Poor sleep increases stress hormones like cortisol, which in turn throw off estrogen and progesterone balance. This can lead to even more sleeping problems, hormone problems, and a cycle that feels hard to break.

**Vitality isn’t just about energy—it’s about resilience.** When women are well-rested, their bodies can better regulate hormone cycles, support immune system strength, and recover from daily stress. This is especially crucial during life phases such as menopause or when facing PMS symptoms.

## Understanding Stress: More Than Just a Feeling

Stress is not just a mental challenge. It’s a physical response, influenced by factors like poor sleep, processed foods, and a lack of movement. Many women notice that when they’re tired or not eating well, their stress levels skyrocket, making hormonal imbalances worse.

**But here’s an empowering truth:**  
Stress can also be a positive force. With guidance from a stress coach or breathing therapist, you can learn to see challenges as opportunities for growth. When you adopt this mindset, your body produces beneficial hormones like DHEA and growth hormone—both crucial for vitality and hormone balance.
