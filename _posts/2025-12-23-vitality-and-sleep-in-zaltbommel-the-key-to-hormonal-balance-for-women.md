## How Vitality and Sleep Affect Hormonal Balance in Zaltbommel

**Hormones** are the body’s internal messengers, influencing everything from energy levels and mood to your menstrual cycle and immune system. When these hormones are balanced, you feel resilient and steady. But when stress, poor sleep, or unhealthy habits disrupt this harmony, symptoms like tiredness, irritability, and irregular periods can quickly appear.

A well-rested body has a better chance of maintaining **hormone balance**. Quality sleep supports essential hormones like melatonin and growth hormone, which help your body recover and repair. Meanwhile, consistent vitality—achieved through balanced living—empowers your body to produce the right hormones at the right times.

## Stress Is Both Physical and Mental

Stress isn’t just “in your head.” It’s a real, physical reaction. Factors such as:

- Lack of restorative sleep
- Skipping nutritious meals in favor of processed foods
- Overcommitting or neglecting relaxation  
can all trigger stress hormones such as cortisol.

But did you know that your mindset towards stress matters? Seeing challenges as opportunities for growth can actually encourage your body to produce beneficial hormones like DHEA and growth hormone, supporting greater resilience and vitality.
