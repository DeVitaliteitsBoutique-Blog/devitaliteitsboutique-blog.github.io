## The Connection Between School Stress, Sleep, and Hormones in The Netherlands

As the school year unfolds, the rhythm of early mornings, homework, and packed schedules can disrupt sleep and increase stress. For many women in the Netherlands, this heightened pace can trigger or worsen symptoms like fatigue, mood swings, PMS, or even irregular cycles. That’s because sleep and hormones are tightly connected:

- **Poor sleep** affects the balance of hormones such as cortisol and melatonin, which play a role in energy, mood, and menstrual cycles.
- **Fatigue** can lead to poor food choices, further impacting hormone health and emotional wellbeing.

Triggers like poor sleep, processed foods, and lack of movement can increase cortisol levels, leaving you feeling drained and anxious.

### How can stress therapy improve hormone health during menopause and PMS?
Stress therapy helps reduce chronic stress that disrupts hormone balance by lowering cortisol levels. Techniques used by stress therapists, including mindfulness and breathing exercises, can alleviate symptoms of menopause and PMS, enhancing overall vitality.
