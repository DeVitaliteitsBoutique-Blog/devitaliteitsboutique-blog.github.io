As sleepcoaches, sleeptherapists, and hormone experts based in The Netherlands, we see every day how sleep and vitality are deeply connected to hormonal health. ### Common Signs of Hormonal Imbalance in Women
- Mood swings and irritability
- Fatigue, especially in the morning
- Difficulty concentrating
- Irregular cycles or intensified PMS
- Trouble falling or staying asleep

## The Stress Connection for Women’s Hormonal Health

Stress is much more than a feeling—it’s a physical and mental reaction to life’s demands. While short-term stress can spark focus and energy, chronic stress keeps the body in a “fight-or-flight” mode. This disrupts hormone balance and weakens the immune system.

Key factors that affect stress and hormones include:
- **Poor sleep**: Increases cortisol, making it harder for your body to recover.
- **Processed foods**: Can spike blood sugar, leading to hormonal fluctuations.
- **Lack of movement**: Reduces the release of positive neurotransmitters and hormones.

But here’s the empowering part: adopting a positive stress mindset—viewing challenges as opportunities—can boost beneficial hormones like DHEA and growth hormone. This shift supports vitality, resilience, and women’s holistic health.
