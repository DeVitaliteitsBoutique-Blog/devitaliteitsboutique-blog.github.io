- Ongoing tiredness or burnout
- Restless nights or insomnia
- Emotional ups and downs
- Irregular menstrual cycles
- Difficulty concentrating

## The Impact of Stress and Lifestyle on Hormones for Women in the Netherlands

Stress is more than a fleeting feeling—it’s both a physical and mental response. When stress lingers, especially with poor sleep or a diet rich in processed foods, it can disrupt hormone production and lead to issues like PMS, menopause discomfort, and sleep and hormone imbalances.

But here’s the empowering part: how you view stress matters. Adopting a positive stress mindset, where challenges are seen as opportunities for growth, can actually boost beneficial hormones such as DHEA and growth hormone. These support your body’s resilience, mood, and hormonal balance.

### Everyday factors that influence your hormones:

- **Sleep quality:** A regular, restful sleep routine allows hormones like melatonin and growth hormone to work effectively.
- **Stress management:** Techniques like breathing exercises calm the nervous system and lower cortisol.
- **Nutrition:** Whole, unprocessed foods nourish your body and stabilize hormone levels.
- **Movement:** Gentle physical activity supports energy, mood, and menstrual regularity.
- **Immune strength:** A strong immune system helps manage inflammation and supports overall vitality.
