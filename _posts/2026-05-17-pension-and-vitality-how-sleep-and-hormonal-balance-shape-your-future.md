## How Sleep Impacts Hormonal Balance

A good night's sleep is more than rest—it's a form of self-care that directly supports your hormonal health. As a sleepcoach and sleeptherapist, we often see how insomnia and sleeping problems disrupt the delicate hormone balance in women. When you don’t sleep well, your body produces more stress hormones like cortisol and less of the beneficial hormones like melatonin, DHEA, and growth hormone. This can lead to:

- Increased fatigue and low energy  
- Mood swings and irritability  
- More severe PMS and menopause symptoms  
- Irregular menstrual cycles  

Prioritizing quality sleep helps stabilize your hormones, leaving you feeling more rested, focused, and positive, whether you’re running your business, caring for your family, or planning your pensioen.

## Stress Is Both Physical and Mental

Stress isn’t just a feeling—it’s a whole-body response. Poor sleep, processed foods, and lack of movement can amplify physical stress, making it harder for your body to recover. Mentally, stress can feel overwhelming, affecting your work-life balance and overall sense of wellbeing.

But stress doesn’t have to be the enemy. With the right support, a positive stress mindset can transform challenges into opportunities for growth. When you embrace stress as a chance to learn and adapt, your body releases more beneficial hormones like DHEA and growth hormone—both essential for vitality and resilience.
