### How Sleep Impacts Hormone Balance

A good night’s sleep is the foundation for healthy hormones. As a **sleepcoach** and **sleeptherapist**, we see that women who improve their sleep often also gain relief from:

- Fatigue and low energy  
- Mood swings and irritability  
- Irregular menstrual cycles  
- Difficulty focusing

During deep sleep, your body restores itself and produces essential hormones like melatonin, DHEA, and growth hormone. These hormones are crucial for repair, emotional balance, and a robust immune system. When sleep is disrupted, your body’s stress response increases, making hormonal issues worse.

### Stress Is More Than a Feeling

Stress is a physical and mental response, not just an emotion. Poor sleep, processed foods, and constant pressure can trigger a stress reaction in your body, leading to elevated cortisol and disturbed hormone balance. But did you know stress can also be positive?

Adopting a positive stress mindset—seeing challenges as opportunities for growth—can actually boost beneficial hormones. DHEA and growth hormone, for example, help your body recover and adapt, building strength and resilience.
