## The Connection Between Sleep, Hormones, and Stress

Sleep is essential for hormone balance. As a sleepcoach and sleeptherapist, we see firsthand how insomnia or restless nights can trigger hormonal problems—especially during menopause or PMS. Poor sleep affects cortisol, DHEA, and growth hormone levels, making it harder to manage stress and maintain a positive mood.

Some common triggers for sleep disturbances and hormone imbalance include:

- **Processed foods and sugar:** These can spike blood sugar and disrupt sleep.
- **Chronic stress:** Both physical and mental stressors increase cortisol, which may worsen hormonal symptoms.
- **Irregular routines:** Changes in daily rhythm, like those caused by stroomstoring Amsterdam, can throw off your sleep-wake cycle.
